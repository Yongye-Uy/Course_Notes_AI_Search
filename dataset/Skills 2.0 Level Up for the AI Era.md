What is a Prompt?

A prompt is simply the instruction or question

you give to AI.

Saves time: Clear instructions mean fewer

revisions and less back-and-forth with AI.

Better results: Produces polished,

consistent, and professional outputs.

Fewer mistakes: Reduces errors,

misunderstandings, and confusion.

11

Crafting Effective Prompts

1. Be clear and concise

2. Use specific examples

3. Vary the prompts

4. Test and refine

5. Use feedback

12

Prompting Techniques

1. Zero-Shot Prompting

Giving instructions only, no examples, and expecting the model to perform the
task correctly

13

2. Few-Shot Prompting

Uses the examples in the prompt as guidance to generate better responses for the next

inputs.  Few-shot can be 1-shot, 3-shot, 5-shot, etc.—more examples can help with more

complex tasks.

14

3. Role Based Prompts

Instead  of  just  giving  an  instruction,  you  tell  the  model  who  it  should  “be”  while

responding. This helps the model adopt the appropriate tone, expertise, or perspective.

15

4. Chain of Thought Technique

Chain-of-Thought  (CoT)  Prompting  is  a  technique  where  a  language  model  is  explicitly

guided to reason step by step before giving a final answer.
